# ICP for Acture AI

## 1. Core ICP (main segment)

**Description:**

VC-backed technology companies in the growth stage (post-product-market fit) with 50–200 employees. These companies face 
challenges with engineering transparency, team coordination, and development predictability—despite using a standard tech stack 
like Jira, Slack, and GitHub.

## 2. Firmographics

- Company size: 50–200 employees
- Engineering team size: 15–80 engineers
- Stage: Growth stage

**Industries:**

- SaaS
- Fintech
- AI

**Geography:**

- San Francisco Bay Area (primary market)
- Singapore (secondary market)

These geographies offer the highest concentration of companies matching our ICP. The San Francisco Bay Area is the world's 
largest hub for venture capital and tech startups, providing the densest pool of potential customers. 
Singapore serves as a regional hub with rapidly growing tech companies and strong investment activity in fintech and AI.

**Why this segment:**

- High concentration of VC-backed startups
- Rapid team growth
- Large engineering teams
- Strong need for process management

## 3. Decision-makers

**Key buyers:**

- CTO / VP of Engineering
- Head of Engineering
- Engineering Manager

**Users:**

- Tech Leads
- Product Managers

**Conclusion:**

Target audience: engineering leadership responsible for delivery and scaling.

## 4. Core Pains

**1. Lack of transparency**

Leaders don't understand what's really happening in their engineering teams. Tools like Jira, Slack, and dashboards provide 
a fragmented and incomplete picture.

**2. High communication overhead**

Teams spend 2–5+ hours per week on coordination, meetings, and manually collecting status updates.

**3. Low development predictability**

It's difficult to assess progress, identify risks, and meet deadlines.

## 5. Current technology stack

- Jira
- GitHub / GitLab
- Slack

**Knowledge & Documentation Systems:**

- Confluence
- Coda
- Notion

This means there are enough data sources, but they need to be interpreted manually.

## 6. Positioning

**Category:**

Narrative layer for engineering data

**Description:**

For engineering leaders in growing teams, Acture AI transforms fragmented engineering activities into clear, 
structured explanations. Unlike dashboards that simply display metrics, it explains what is actually happening.

## 7. Competitive advantage

**BI tools (Tableau, Power BI):**

- Require manual configuration
- Show metrics only
- Provide no context

**Dev analytics (LinearB, Jellyfish):**

- Provide metrics (velocity, cycle time)
- Require interpretation

**Acture AI:**

- Automatically generates explanations of what's happening
- Converts data into meaning

Key shift: from metrics to meaning

## 8. Psychographics

**Typical mindset (based on interviews):**

> "We lack real communication between devs, QA, and analysts.
> And leadership often isn’t technical enough to fully understand what’s going on."

> "We’re constantly trying to balance speed with quality while solving business problems from the engineering side."

> "There’s little initiative in communication — people don’t proactively share context."

> "To understand what’s really happening, we have to ping people on Slack or jump on quick calls. Jira is supposed to help,
> but it doesn’t reflect reality. There’s no single source of truth."

**Typical patterns:**

- Distrust of data (Jira ≠ reality)
- Sense of losing control
- Need for constant manual verification

**Behavior:**

- Frequent meetings
- Heavy reliance on Slack
- No single source of truth
- Communication breakdowns among team members

## 9. Purchase triggers

A client is ready for implementation when:

- The team has grown from 20 to 50+ engineers
- Multiple teams have been formed
- Deadline issues have emerged
- Process transparency has declined

**Key trigger:**

Accelerated development—often driven by AI adoption—has reduced clarity into what's actually happening.

## 10. Conclusion

Acture AI targets VC-backed tech companies from key startup hubs (San Francisco Bay Area and Singapore) with teams of 
50–200 people that are in a rapid growth phase and facing a loss of transparency in engineering processes. 
The product bridges the gap between data availability and data understanding, transforming engineering activity 
into structured and interpretable reports for decision-making.
